Folio 183 Recto
Compare Witnesses: •
Now in the name / of my lord Ihesus •
of right hole herte / in all my best entent •
my lyf remembryng / froward and vicious •
Aye contrarie / to the comaundement •
of christ Ihesu / nowe with avisement •
the lord besechyng / to have mercy and piete •
my youthe mynne age / how þat I have spent •
with this word seid / knelynge on my kne. •
O Jhesu mercy / with support of thy grace •
for thy meke passioun / remembre on my compla[e]ynt1 •
duryng my lyff / withe many grete trespace •
by many A wrong path / wher I have myswent •
I now purpose / by thy grace influent •
to wryt A tretys / of surfetes done to the •
and call it / my last testament •
to with Ihesu mers mercy2 / knelyng on my kne. •
explicit prologus of Iohnn lidgats testament3 •
Notes
  1. The scribe appears to have replaced an "a" with an "e" here. ↩
  2. The scribe appears to have begun to write "mersy" and then corrected themselves, repeating with "mercy" ↩
  3. "explicit prologus of" appears to be a later addition over erased text -- the letters "th" can be seen to the left of "explicit" but the rest cannot be made out clearly by sight alone. ↩
