Folio 180 Recto
Compare Witnesses: •
In amorous hertes / brennyng of kyndenesse •
this name Ihesu / most profoundly dothe myne •
Martir1 Ignacius / can beren ther of witnesse •
amyd whos herte / by grace which is dyvyne •
with aureat lettres / as gold that dyd shyne •
his herte was grave / men may his legende se •
to blissed Ihesu / and bowe A doun ther kne. •
This is the name / that chaceth away the clips •
of foreyn dirknesse / as clerkes determyne •
by Johnn remembrid / in thapocalips •
how lyke a lambe / his hede he did enclyne •
whos blode downe ran / right as any lyne •
to wasshe the ordures / of owr Inyquyte •
medled with water / clere and cristallyne •
which from his herte / doun railled by his kne. •
By blode Ihesu / made owr redempcioun •
with water of baptem / from felthe wh2 wesshe vs clene •
and from his herte / two licours ther rann3 doun •
on calvary / the truthe was well sene •
whann that longious / with A spere kene •
perced his hert / vpon the rode tre •
O mann vn4 onkynde / thynk what this dothe mene •
and onto Ihesu / bowe adoun the A thy kne. •
Ther is no speche / nor language cann remembre •
lettre, sillable / nor word þat may expresse •
though in to tunges / wer turned every membre •
Of man to telle / thexcellent noblesse •
Of blissed Ihesu / whiche of his grete mekenesse •
list suffre deth / to make his servaunt fre •
nowe mercyfull Ihesu / for thyne high godnesse •
have mercy on all / that bowe to the ther kne. •
Notes
  1. The descender at the end of the "m" glyph here appears to be how the scribe indicates a magiscule rather than miniscule letter. Compare it with "Master in the incipit of the manuscript on folio 179v. ↩
  2. Here the scribe made an error, which they have corrected with a strikethrough before continuing. ↩
  3. This is unlikely to be an otiose mark as final "n" in a word does not have an idiose mark in the same word in line five above (albeit there with the single "n" spelling rather than the double "n" implied here). ↩
  4. It appears that the scribe began to write some form of "vnkind" here before correcting with "onkynde." Harley 218 has "vnkende" here. ↩
