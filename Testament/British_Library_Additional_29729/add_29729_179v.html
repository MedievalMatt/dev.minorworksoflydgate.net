This folowynge is þe prologe of Johnn Lydgattes •
testament which I fownd in Master stantons boke •
O howe holsum and glad / is the memorie •
of crist Ihesu / surmountyng all swetnesse •
name of conquest / of triumphe and victorie •
thassaunt of sathan / to venquysshe and oppresse •
to which name / seynt poule bereth1 witnesse •
of hevene and erthe / and infernall pouste •
alle creatures of right / and dewe humblesse •
and of hole herte / bowe shall ther kne. •
No song so sote / onto the audience •
as is Ihesus / nor so full of plesaunce •
a geyn alle enemyes / sheld pavis and defence •
to hevy hertes / chieff counfort in substaunce •
of gostly gladnesse / most counfort in substaunce2su ffisaunce •
chief directorie / to hevene ward the cite •
gladdest resort / of spirituall remembraunce •
to whome alle creatures / bowe shall ther kne. •
To all folkes / that stonde in repentaunce •
with herte contrite / made ther confessioun3 •
of will and thought / accomplisshed ther penaunce •
and to ther power / done satisfaccioun •
[that clayme by mene / of christes passioun4] •
marked with tav ·Τ·5 for more suerte •
to them Ihesu / shall graunte full pardoun •
to aske hym mercy / whan6they knele on ther kne. •
In this name Ihesu / most sovereyn of vertu •
stant all owr hope / and all owr assuraunce •
for wher that evar / named is Ihesu •
geyn gostly trouble / men fynde alleggeaunce •
who trusteth Ihesu / may fele no grevaunce •
which from all thraldam7 / brought vs to liberte •
owt of servage / he made acquyntaunce •
to all that knele / to Ihesu on ther kne. •
Notes
  1. The "t" rather than the "h" glyph appears barred, indicating a suspension. ↩
  2. The scribe has made an error here and concluded with "substaunce," which is the concluding word of the previous line. The correct word, "suffisaunce," is written following but is partially obscured by a blot on the manuscript page. The descenders of the two "f" glyphs can be seen below the blot, however, and closer inspection shows some indication of the intended strokes underneath the blot. ↩
  3. The curved line over the n here is intended as a suspension mark when compared with other glyphs in the manuscript--for example the terminal "n"'s in "passion" and "pardoun" in the same stanza. ↩
  4. The scribe realized they'd forgotten this line some time after they wrote line six of this verse, and so it is shoehorned between four and six on the page. ↩
  5. This is one of several witnesses where the scribe has not only the word "tau," but a representation of the letter next to it. It also replaces the virgule in this line. ↩
  6. There is a mark over the "n" glyph here, but when compared with other suspensions in the manuscript it appears to be otiose. ↩
  7. The form of the second-to-last glyph is an "a" rather than an "o" when compared with other uses of both "a" and "o" throughout. ↩
