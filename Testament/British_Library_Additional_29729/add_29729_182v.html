Folio 182 Verso
Compare Witnesses: •
do mercy Ihesu / or we hens passe •
owt of this perillous / dredefull pilgrymage •
besette with brigauntes / leyd wayte in every place •
with mortall saute / to lette owr passage •
Amonge othar / I that ame falle in age •
gretely feblysshed / of olde infirmyte •
crye onto Ihesu / for my synfull outrage •
Right of hole herte / thus knelyng on my kne. •
Lat not be lost / that thou has bought so dere •
with gold nor silver / but with thy precious blode •
owr flessh is freill / but short abydynge here •
the olde serpent / malicious and wode •
the worlde vnstable / nowe ebbe nowe is flode •
of1eche thynge concludyng / on mutabilitie •
geynn whos daungers / I holde this counsayll goode •
to praye for mercy / to Ihesu on owr kne. •
And vnder support / Ihesu of thy favour •
or I passe hens / this hoolly myne entent •
to make Ihesu / to be chief surveyour •
of my laste will / sette in my testament •
which of my self / am Insuffient2 •
to rekene or counte / but mercy and piete •
be prefferryd / or thou do Iugement •
to all that call / to Ihesu on ther kne. •
Age is crope In / callythe me to my grave •
to make rekenyng / howe I my tyme have spent •
bareynn of vertue / allas who shall me save •
fro fendes daunger / tacounte for my talent •
but Ihesu be my staffe / and my potent •
overstreyght audyt / is like tencoumbre me •
or dome be yove / but mercy be present •
to all that knele / to Ihesu on ther kne. •
Notes
  1. It appears that the scribe began this line with the word "of," but realized their mistake and deleted it. ↩
  2. The scribe appears to have skipped the "ic" of "insufficient" here. ↩
