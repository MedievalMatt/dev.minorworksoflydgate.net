Nowe feloweth here the maner of a bille by weye •
of supplycation put to the kynge holdinge his noble •
fest of crystmasse in the castell of hartford as in •
dysguysinge of þe rude vpplandishe people complayninge •
on hir wyues / with the boystrus answere of thiyr1 •
wyues / deuysed by lidgate / at þe requeste of the •
count rowlore / Brys slayn at louiers •
Notes
  1. The letter forms are slightly worn, but it appears that the scribe started writing 'thie' before deciding to replace the 'e' with a 'y.' I'm not positive that is what is going on at this point, so I have left it as "thiyr." ↩
