Which at the cost and contemplacion •
of a Iantylman1 •
thrughe the labour / and meditacion •
of dampun Johnm Thornton / ye shall vnderstonde •
Archedecon here / whiche out of Englonde •
went ot Rome / and this pardon did purchace •
christe2 blesse them both / with holy honde •
and for theyr labor taked take them to his grace •
Explicit A balad of þe Image of our •
lady made by Johnn lidgate •
Notes
  1. The EETS edition has "gentylman," but it's clear the initial glyph is a majuscule I ↩
  2. the initial glyphs are the greek letters chi and rho, meant to be a shorthand for Christ. ↩
