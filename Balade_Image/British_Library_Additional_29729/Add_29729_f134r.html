A balad made by Iohn lydgat of1 þe ymage of •
our lady •
behold and se / this glorious fygure •
whiche sent luke / of our lady lyvynge •
aftar her lyknes / made in picture •
lo here she is / aftar the same wyrkynge •
as in Rome is had / of saynt lukes payntynge •
in erthe as she was / and her sonne also •
and ther honoryd / with solempne praysynge •
In churche callyd / maria de populo •
who devotly visitith the same fygure •
In worshipe of our moste blessyd lady fre •
of .v. Cardinals perpetuelly to endure •
.v. C yeres of Remyssyon graunted be •
of penaunce Ingoynyd / of he have capacite •
pardon to receyve / contrite with confession •
with satysfacion / this is the certence •
as vnder sealys / the bulle make mencion •
At certayn festis in the same bulle2 comprisyd •
firste of our lady the Assumpcion •
Anuncion3 also / it is ther in devisyd •
Of sent Albon the passyon and his invencion •
of this churche4 allso5 the dedycacion •
at every feste who visytythe this fygure here •
of penaunce Ingoyned hath Relaxacon6 •
of everi Cardinall grauntyd a .C. yere •
Nomber of þe yeres / who lyst to accompte •
that be grauntyd .v. tymes in the yere •
to .m. and .vC. therto they amounte •
to eche creature with devoute prayer •
that helpe this place / with some thynge particuler7 •
as boke belle Shalis8 / or any oder9 guerdion •
they be copertenars of this same pardoun •
Notes
  1. There is what appears to be a corrected letter to the left of "of here, but the correction mark is a plus rather than what we see elsewhere. ↩
  2. The EETS edition has "buke" here, but when compared with the final line above and the "k" glyph as seen in the third and fourth lines of the first stanza it is evident it's actually a doubled "l." ↩
  3. The EETS edition has "n" in positions two and three, but the number of minims make it clear that this is not possible. ↩
  4. The scribe made a mistake and crossed it out with an "X," but it's not entirely clear what the crossed letter or letters initially were. ↩
  5. The EETS edition has only a single "l," but it's clear in the manuscript that the letter is in fact doubled. ↩
  6. The EETS edition has "cion," but there is no "i" in the manuscript. ↩
  7. The EETS edition has "particular" here, but the manuscript makes it clear that it's "er" rather than "ar" at the end. ↩
  8. The EETS edition has "chalis" but what would be the scribe's majuscule "c" form does not match what we see here. Instead, it's much closer to the terminal "s" form. ↩
  9. The EETS edition has "other" here. ↩
