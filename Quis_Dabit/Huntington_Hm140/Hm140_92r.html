Folio 92 Recto
Compare Witnesses: •
This is the name that chasith a way the clipse1 •
Of foreyn dyvknes as clerkys determayne •
Oy Jhesu remembred in tha pocalippas •
Howe liche a lambe his hed he did enclyne •
Whose blode downe ran as right as any lyne •
To wassh the ordures of oure iniquite •
Medlyd with water clere crystallins •
Which from his hert downe ran by his knee •
Ther is no speech nor langage can remember •
Letter sillabe nor word that may expresse •
Thrugh in to tungis were turnyd euery membre •
Of man to tell the xcellent noblese •
Of blessid jhesu which of his grete mekenesse •
Lyst suffer dethe to make his servant free •
Now mercyfull Ihesu for thyn high godenesse •
Haue mercy on all that bowe to the there knee •
The prince was slayne the servant went at large •
And to delyver his subget from prisoun •
The lord toke on hym for to bere the charge •
To quyte man kynde by obligacioun •
Sealled with v woundes to pay oure raunsomme •
Man to restore to paradise his Cite •
Is manne not bounde I aske the question •
To blessid Ihesu for to bowe his knee •
Syx hundreth tymes with sexty tolde in number •
I powles pystels Ihesu men may rede •
Multitude of fendes to encoumber •
To pay oure raunsome his blode he dud shed •
Not a scmale partie bot he did blede •
Notes
  1. This stanza appears as number seen here, but is number six in the order provided by MacCracken in EETS 107. ↩
