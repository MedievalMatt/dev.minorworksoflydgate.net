Folio 67 Recto
Compare Witnesses: •
¶
Who shal of wepyng / gif me suffisaunce •
or to my sobbyng / who shal me teerys fynde •
to see my Joye / myn hertys hiħ plesaunce •
my soote sone / that was to me so kynde •
Rayled with reed blood / as sap doth thoruħ þe rynde •
thoruh his enmyes / vengable crueltee •
derkenyd with deeth / his eyen now maad blynde •
attwix too theevys / nailed to a tree •
¶
My Joye / my liħt / my lanterne moost enteere •
This heuenly phebus is clipsid of his liħt •
This Esperus / hath hid his beemys cleere •
And is of newe / certeyned fro my sight •
whan shal his day sterre / shewe me his bemys briħt •
to cleeve the trouble of myn adversitee •
parde the Jewys don me to gret vnriħt •
to nayle my sone / allas vnto a tree •
¶
O alle ye douħtren of ierusaleem •
Ha som compassioun / on my siħħes deepe •
nat lik the gladnesse / which I had in Bedleem •
cometh neer of routhe / & helith me for to weepe •
a swerd of deth doth thoruħ myn herte creepe •
I feele it ful weel / of moodirly pitee •
Craunpisshed / with swownyng I do sleepe •
To see my sone / thus nailed to a tree •
